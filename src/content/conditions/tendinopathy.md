---
name: "Tendinopathy"
aliases: ["tendinitis", "tennis elbow", "lateral epicondylitis", "Achilles tendinopathy", "overuse tendon pain"]
summary: "Pain and dysfunction of a tendon from overuse — common at the elbow (tennis elbow), Achilles, and shoulder — where the tendon degenerates rather than simply 'inflames'."
bodyParts: ["forearm", "upper-arm"]
severity: "chronic"
redFlags:
  - "A sudden 'pop' or snap with immediate loss of strength or function — possible tendon rupture; needs urgent assessment."
  - "Joint warmth, redness, and swelling with fever — possible infection rather than overuse."
  - "Constant pain that wakes you from sleep, or pain unrelated to activity — atypical for mechanical tendinopathy."
  - "Recent fluoroquinolone antibiotic or corticosteroid use with new tendon pain — raised rupture risk."
order: 36
---

The mainstay of tendinopathy care is progressive loading exercise (eccentric / heavy-slow resistance),
not pills. The one supplement with supportive — though preliminary — evidence is collagen peptides
taken before loading, which appears to build tendon tissue but has not yet been shown to relieve
tendon *pain*.
