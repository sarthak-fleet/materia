# Competitive analysis & positioning

Condensed from the 2026-06 landscape research. Positioning rationale; see `decision-log.md` for the
decisions this drove.

## The white space

The two halves of "what can I take for this?" exist in separate products that never meet:

| Product | Has | Missing |
|---|---|---|
| **BioDigital Human** | interactive anatomy → condition → treatment | zero herb/supplement data; B2B/clinical |
| **Examine.com** | A–F evidence grades per supplement × outcome; ad-free, no supplement money | no anatomy, no body-part entry |
| **NatMed Pro** | rigorous herb×drug interaction data | paywalled, clinical, dated UI |
| **MSK About Herbs / NCCIH** | free, cited herb monographs | narrow; no grades scale; no anatomy |
| **TCM/acupuncture apps** | body + remedy-layer UX | tradition-only, no evidence grading |
| **InnerBody** | anatomy + supplements together | funded by selling its own supplements (conflicted) |
| **WebMD / Healthline** | huge consumer reach, SEO | ad/affiliate-driven; shallow rigor |

**Nobody owns the join:** a body-part entry point that resolves to evidence-graded, cited remedies,
ad-free, in a premium content-first experience.

## Positioning

> **The Examine.com of the whole body** — explore an interactive body, click any part, and see
> evidence-graded, fully-cited herbs, supplements, and drugs, connected by a knowledge graph linking
> body → condition → remedy → active compound → mechanism → study.

## The moats Materia is building

1. **The anatomy → condition → evidence-graded-remedy join** — the unoccupied white space.
2. **A traversable knowledge graph with active compounds as first-class nodes** — structurally beats
   every siloed competitor and powers a combinatorial SEO long-tail.
3. **A free consumer herb-AND-drug safety checker** — the one rigorous tool (herb×drug) that today is
   locked behind NatMed Pro's clinical paywall.
4. **Tradition-vs-evidence overlay** — honest Ayurveda/TCM/Western claims beside the modern grade.
5. **Research-paper / study-level transparency** — study design, n, PMID/DOI on every claim.

## Trust & legal posture

Mirror Examine: ad-free, no supplement sales, no money from companies whose products we describe —
which is also the lowest-liability stance (selling/affiliate-linking would make us an "advertiser"
with FTC claim-substantiation duties). Grade conservatively, cite everything, gate red-flag
conditions, and describe studied amounts rather than prescribing. Disclaimers are prominent and
unavoidable.

Grading convention: GRADE-inspired *certainty* under the hood, Examine-style consumer letter
(A/B/C/D/insufficient) on top, graded per remedy × condition, efficacy and safety separate.
